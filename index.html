Valenran

Valenran was born under the canopy of the lush forest, surrounded by the whispers of the ancient trees. As a child, he was insatiably curious, always eager to unravel the mysteries of the world around him. At 30 years old, Valenran underwent rigorous Wood Elf Training with his father, mastering the ways of stealth, archery, and pathfinding.

But it was his connection to the goddess Nocturnal that truly set him apart from his kin. Nocturnal, the protector of the shadows, had captured Valenran's heart and soul, and he was drawn to her teachings like a moth to a flame. His parents, devout followers of the forest god Silvanus, were initially hesitant to indulge Valenran's unusual interests. But they could see the passion burning within him and allowed him to delve deeper into the teachings of Nocturnal.

Valenran spent countless hours studying the rituals and practices of the goddess, mastering the art of sneaking and hiding until he was a shadow in the forest. His thirst for knowledge was unquenchable, and so his parents eventually allowed him to travel to a nearby monastery, where he hoped to learn more about Nocturnal's teachings under the guidance of monks devoted to her worship. At the monastery, Valenran found a true home and connection with the head monk, Hierophant Arkus, who became his mentor. Arkus nurtured Valenran's devotion and taught him the martial arts and ways of discipline. Valenran flourished under Arkus's tutelage, but his yearning for knowledge was still insatiable. The monks welcomed Valenran, under the condition that the secrets of Nocturnal are only shared with the Elder Monks: Valenran was bound to their oath.

Valenran's friend, a young monk named Dathius, warned him not to delve into the forbidden knowledge, but Valenran would not be deterred. He snuck into the library, devouring the contents of the forbidden texts, and mastering the art of the "Way of the Shadow".

His transgressions were eventually uncovered by Hierophant Arkus, who was livid at Valenran's disregard for the rules. The punishment was swift and harsh, and Valenran was exiled from the monastery, forced to say a painful goodbye to his mentor Arkus and his friend Dathius. But Valenran was not one to be deterred by such minor setbacks. He absconded with the forbidden texts and continued to practice his newly acquired skills in the art of shadow.

Now, Valenran roams the world, his martial arts and shadowy skills at the ready, always ready to come to the aid of those in need. He follows Nocturnal, but he has also learned to appreciate the delicate balance between light and dark, striving to maintain a true neutral stance in all things. After all, in a world of darkness and light, it is those who can move seamlessly between the two who hold the true power.

After leaving the monastery, Valenran found himself in a desert oasis city where he encountered Kadryn, a shrewd merchant who saw potential in Valenran's skills. Under Kadryn's watchful eye, Valenran joined a diverse group of gladiators and mercenaries, each with their own unique abilities and motivations. Over time, he formed a bond with his new companions: a Kenku Hexblade, a Changeling Hexblade, a Bugbear Samurai, and a Halfling Bard.

As he fought alongside his new allies, Valenran's skills grew, and he developed a deep respect for the tactical precision of their teamwork. Though he continued to follow Nocturnal's teachings, Valenran couldn't help but feel a sense of camaraderie with the other gladiators. He honed his martial arts skills in the arena, learning from their diverse fighting styles and adapting to suit his own abilities.

Chapter 1: Assembly of the Party

Our journey began in an oasis city, where fate had drawn together an unlikely band of individuals: Neremyn Valenran, a Wood Elf Monk dedicated to Nocturnal's shadowy teachings; Nock, a Kenku Hexblade under the service of Slaanesh, the Chaos God of Pleasure and Excess; Baron Calvisius Von Maximus, a Changeling Hexblade with a keen wit bound to the enigma of The Traveler; Fenwick, a Halfling Bard with a love for music and a knack for intrigue; and Eex Glump, a Bugbear Fighter with a past steeped in adversity and a heart brimming with courage and loyalty. Each with their own complicated histories, fate had eventually brought them all to Kadryn, a prestigious merchant from the city; and together they worked as his champions in the cityâ€™s arena in exchange for food, shelter, equipment, and coin.

Chapter 2: A Favor for Kadryn

Soon after their meeting, the party was approached by Kadryn, the kind merchant who had previously given refuge to Eex Glump before eventually accepting the rest of the party into his home. Kadryn was indebted to some dwarves he knew, and in turn, requested the party's assistance to repay this debt. The task? To locate an ancient gemstone, more myth than fact, said to wield control over wind and sand. The dwarves, eager to lay their hands on this legendary artifact, offered magical items in return.

Chapter 3: Heist from the Southwinds

The party's search for the gemstone led them to the underwater offices of the Southwinds TradingCompany. Disguised as employees, they infiltrated the office, pilfering the gemstone from under the noses of unsuspecting researchers and confining it within a briefcase of holding. Their successful heist was not without repercussions. As the city quaked under the onset of a massive sandstorm, the likes of which had not been seen for centuries. The city's magical protections crumbled under the ferocity of the storm, causing chaos and panic among its inhabitants.

Chapter 4: Ambush and Aftermath

In the midst of the turmoil, the party was ambushed by a formidable security detail from the Southwinds Trading Company. Despite the odds, they managed to overcome their assailants, even going so far as to ruthlessly eliminate a fleeing member of the security team in an attempt to leave no witnesses. Upon reaching the embassy and exchanging the gemstone for their promised magical items, the party was taken aback by the dwarves' odd behavior. The dwarves sequestered themselves in a separate room, their expressions a mix of gratitude and unease. The party was left to ponder the true power of the gemstone they had just handed over.

Chapter 5: City's Downfall and New Bonds

The party's return was met with a city on the brink of collapse. Sandstorms raged relentlessly, burying the once thriving oasis city under heaps of sand. Even the city's most skilled spellcasters struggled to hold back the storm's wrath. The party managed to find Kadryn at the arena, amidst other city leaders, all grappling with the crisis at hand. Despite their wish to stay with him, Kadryn's insistence on waiting for his wife's family, and the imminent danger of the city being buried, forced the party to make a difficult decision. They left Kadryn and the crumbling city behind, their hearts laden with guilt and sorrow.

Chapter 6: The Journey Eastward

Guided by their respective deities, the party ventured eastward towards a port-town so that they could travel to the Elven lands. They joined hundreds of people fleeing the city, their journey hampered by sand elementals and lesser sand fiends that attacked escaping civilians. The party fought off these creatures, ensuring the safety of those around them before continuing their journey to the nearest settlement with a port.

Chapter 7: A New Beginning

Upon their arrival, they learned of the city's destruction being blamed on the Southwinds Trading Company. Choosing to remain silent about their involvement, the party quickly boarded a merchant ship, agreeing to work as crewmates in exchange for passage north towards the Elven lands.

Chapter 8: Voyage to the Islands

After escaping the crumbling city, the party quickly boarded a merchant ship bound for the Elven lands. However, their journey would first take them through a group of islands where the merchants planned to conduct trade with the local inhabitants, including a Furbolg innkeeper. Their voyage was not without perils. A few days into their journey, the ship was attacked by a pod of dreadfins - monstrous, shark-like sea creatures. Despite the harrowing encounter, the party managed to fend off the creatures, saving the ship and its crew from certain doom. The experience was a grim reminder of the dangers they would have to face on their journey.

Chapter 9: Arrival at the Islands

Their journey at sea culminated in their arrival at the islands. However, they were met with unsettling news: the Furbolg innkeeper, a key figure in the merchant's trade plans, was missing. The captain, eager to get paid, tasked the party with finding the Furbolg. What was supposed to be a simple search and rescue mission quickly escalated into a series of adventures. The party found themselves battling hags, hosting a barbecue for island natives, and even combating a plague-based cult. They eventually found the Furbolg innkeeper, along with several other prisoners and a quartet of giant baby turtles. The party had to make the difficult decision to eliminate a few of the prisoners who were infected with the plague, which apparently had the terrifying effect of transforming its victims into demonic plague-creatures.

Chapter 10: Uncovering Prophecies and a New Threat

Amidst the chaos, the party found a book containing a prophecy of an impending apocalyptic event. According to the prophecy, avatars of the seven deadly sins would make themselves known to the world, heralding a time of great upheaval. This revelation cast a shadow over their victory, hinting at the dire challenges they would face in the future. The party managed to return to the merchant ship & the main island with the Furbolg and the other prisoners. After some much needed rest and recovery, the Furbolg was finally in a condition to conduct trade with the merchant ship. The ship captain got paid, and as such, the party got paid. As they prepared to leave the islands, they spotted a new, unmapped island in the distance. The bard recognized it as "High Ground," a colossal turtle she used to travel and adventure with. The party decided to venture towards High Ground, but as they neared the colossal creature, they were confronted by yet another colossal creature, a shark-like beast of menacing proportions.

Chapter 11: Battle with the Avatar of Gluttony

The creature, revealed to be the current Avatar of Gluttony, one of the seven deadly sins mentioned in the prophecy, charged at the boat with its mouth wide open. The party launched a barrage of ranged attacks against it, but to no avail. Just as it was about to reach the boat, the creature shrank and violently flipped the boat, leaving the fate of the party hanging in balance.
